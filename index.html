Terminology Glossary
What do the tier labels mean?
Hover labels throughout the dashboard to see definitions inline — or reference below
Standard
Full-price community members. NAWS's core client base. This tier held flat year-over-year — no community demand loss.
Rescue (Licensed Orgs)
Licensed animal rescue organizations that receive discounted surgical pricing. The biggest driver of 2025's decline — down 30.6%.
NQ — Not Qualifying
Low-income community members who receive reduced-cost services based on financial need. Relatively stable year-over-year (−7.3%).
SNCKC
Spay Neuter Kansas City — a regional partnership program for Kansas City-area animals. Down 22.8% in 2025.
Feral / Ear Tip
TNR (Trap-Neuter-Return) program for community/feral cats. Ear tipping identifies already-neutered cats. Up 2.4% in 2025.
NQ vs. Rescue
NQ serves individual low-income people. Rescue serves organizations. Both receive discounts, but through different eligibility criteria.

Key Findings

Critical Risk
Rescue Partnership Collapse
−30.6%
Rescue org surgeries fell from 1,177 → 816, accounting for 81% of the entire 2025 volume decline. In 2024, rescue orgs represented 28.6% of all surgeries. This is a structural dependency risk — without intervention, it threatens long-term program revenue stability.
🐕
Critical Risk
Canine Surgery Collapse
−26.2%
Canine surgeries dropped from 1,017 → 751 — directly tied to rescue partner decline since rescue orgs disproportionately serve dogs. Feline surgeries fell only 5.0%, confirming canines are the concentrated risk area requiring targeted outreach.
📉
Warning
Q4 Seasonal Weakness
846 in Q4
Q4 2025 was the weakest quarter, with December bottoming at 239 surgeries — the year's lowest. No October surge materialized (2024 peak: 412 → 2025: 318). A targeted year-end campaign could recover 80–110 procedures and ~$6,000 in revenue.
🤝
Warning
SNCKC Volume Erosion
−22.8%
SNCKC partnership declined from 101 → 78 surgeries — a secondary warning signal. While small in absolute terms, a continued decline could mirror the larger rescue org pattern. Proactive relationship management is recommended now, before further slippage.
💪
Strength
Community Demand Unchanged
1,996 = 1,996
The Standard tier held perfectly flat at 1,996 surgeries, proving community demand is entirely healthy. The NAWS brand has not weakened in the community. All volume losses are on the institutional/partnership side — a solvable, targeted problem.
💰
Strength
Revenue Efficiency Preserved
$63.48 / surgery
Revenue per procedure rose 0.6% ($63.07 → $63.48), confirming zero margin erosion. The revenue shortfall is 100% volume-driven — not a pricing or operational efficiency problem. This gives NAWS full pricing flexibility to pursue optimizations.
