Rescue Partnership Decline
−361 surgeries
Rescue org volume dropped 30.6% (1,177 → 816) — the single biggest driver of the 2025 overall decline. Standard and Low Income tiers held steady. Canine rescue surgeries were hit hardest.
✅
Pricing Efficiency Held
$63.48 / surgery
Revenue per procedure increased 0.6% ($63.07 → $63.48). The revenue decline is 100% volume-driven — there is zero pricing or margin erosion.
🎯
Recovery Target
+37 surgeries/month
To match 2024's total of 4,109 surgeries, NAWS needs 445 additional procedures — approximately 37 more per month, or 111 per quarter. Reactivating 2–3 rescue partnerships would close this gap.
🦊
Community TNR Growing
885 feral cats
Feral cat Trap-Neuter-Return (TNR) surgeries rose 2.4% (873 → 894), bucking the overall trend. Community cat programming is strengthening even as rescue org volume falls.

Terminology Glossary
What do the tier labels mean?
Hover labels throughout the dashboard to see definitions inline — or reference below
Standard
Full-price community members. NAWS's core client base. This tier held flat year-over-year — no community demand loss.
Rescue (Licensed Orgs)
Licensed animal rescue organizations that receive discounted surgical pricing. The biggest driver of 2025's decline — down 30.6%.
NQ — Not Qualifying
Low-income community members who receive reduced-cost services based on financial need. Relatively stable year-over-year (−7.3%).
SNCKC
Spay Neuter Kansas City — a regional partnership program for Kansas City-area animals. Down 22.8% in 2025.
Feral / Ear Tip
TNR (Trap-Neuter-Return) program for community/feral cats. Ear tipping identifies already-neutered cats. Up 2.4% in 2025.
NQ vs. Rescue
NQ serves individual low-income people. Rescue serves organizations. Both receive discounts, but through different eligibility criteria.
